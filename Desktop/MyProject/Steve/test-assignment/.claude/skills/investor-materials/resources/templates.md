## Financial Model Templates

### Pre-Seed Financial Model (Layer-by-Layer)

```
ASSUMPTIONS (BASE CASE)

Starting Point
• Starting MRR: $12,000
• Starting customers: 40
• ARPU: $300/month

Growth
• MoM growth: 15% (months 1-12), declining to 10% (months 13-18)
• Churn: 5% monthly
• Expansion revenue: 10% of existing customers upgrade annually

Unit Economics
• CAC: $2,500
• LTV: $36,000 (24 months × $300/month, 50% gross margin)
• LTV:CAC = 14.4x
• CAC payback: 6 months
```

```
REVENUE MODEL (18 MONTHS)

Layer 1: Starting Customers (Retention)
= Starting customers × Retention rate × ARPU

Month 1: 40 × 0.95 × $300 = $11,400
Month 6: 40 × 0.95^6 × $300 = $8,377
Month 12: 40 × 0.95^12 × $300 = $6,150
Month 18: 40 × 0.95^18 × $300 = $4,520

Layer 2: New Customer Acquisition
= Marketing spend ÷ CAC × Conversion rate

Month 1: ($10,000 ÷ $2,500) × 0.8 = 3 customers × $300 = $900
Month 6: ($15,000 ÷ $2,500) × 0.8 = 5 customers × $300 = $1,500
Month 12: ($25,000 ÷ $2,500) × 0.8 = 8 customers × $300 = $2,400
Month 18: ($40,000 ÷ $2,500) × 0.8 = 13 customers × $300 = $3,900

Layer 3: Expansion Revenue
= Existing customers × 10% annual upgrade ÷ 12 months × $100 uplift

Month 1: 40 × 0.0083 × $100 = $33
Month 6: 50 × 0.0083 × $100 = $42
Month 12: 90 × 0.0083 × $100 = $75
Month 18: 160 × 0.0083 × $100 = $133

Layer 4: Churn Impact
= Total customers × Churn rate × ARPU

Month 1: 43 × 0.05 × $300 = $645
Month 6: 58 × 0.05 × $300 = $870
Month 12: 100 × 0.05 × $300 = $1,500
Month 18: 180 × 0.05 × $300 = $2,700

TOTAL REVENUE = Layer 1 + Layer 2 + Layer 3 - Layer 4

Month 1: $11,400 + $900 + $33 - $645 = $11,688
Month 6: $8,377 + $1,500 + $42 - $870 = $10,049
Month 12: $6,150 + $2,400 + $75 - $1,500 = $7,125
Month 18: $4,520 + $3,900 + $133 - $2,700 = $5,853

Wait — this shows DECLINING revenue due to churn math error.
FIX: Growth should add net new customers AFTER churn.

CORRECTED MODEL:
Net New Customers = (Marketing Spend ÷ CAC × Conversion) - (Existing × Churn)

Month 1: (3 - 2) = +1 customer → 41 total × $300 = $12,300
Month 6: (5 - 3) = +2 customers → 55 total × $300 = $16,500
Month 12: (8 - 5) = +3 customers → 95 total × $300 = $28,500
Month 18: (13 - 9) = +4 customers → 175 total × $300 = $52,500
```

```
EXPENSE MODEL (18 MONTHS)

HEADCOUNT PLAN

Months 1-6 (Seed)
• CEO: $8,000/mo
• CTO: $8,000/mo
• Backend Dev: $7,000/mo
• Frontend Dev: $7,000/mo
Total: 4 people × $7,500 × 1.2 (payroll tax) = $36,000/mo

Months 7-12
• +2 Engineers: $7,000/mo each
• +1 SDR: $5,000/mo
Total: 7 people × $6,714 × 1.2 = $57,000/mo

Months 13-18
• +2 Engineers: $7,000/mo each
• +3 SDRs: $5,000/mo each
• +1 AE: $6,000/mo
Total: 13 people × $6,308 × 1.2 = $98,000/mo

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)
• Hosting (AWS): $2,000/mo → $5,000/mo (scale)
• Payment processing (Stripe): Revenue × 2.9% = $350 → $1,500/mo
• Support tools: $500/mo
Total COGS: $2,850 → $7,000/mo

OPERATIONS
• Office (WeWork): $3,000/mo
• Tools (Notion, Slack, Linear): $1,000/mo
• Legal (formation, IP): $15,000 one-time
• Accounting: $1,000/mo
Total Ops: $5,000/mo (months 1-6), $6,000/mo (months 7-18)

MARKETING
• Content: $2,000/mo
• Events/conferences: $5,000/mo
• Ads: $5,000/mo
Total Marketing: $12,000/mo (months 1-6), $25,000/mo (months 7-18)

TOTAL MONTHLY BURN

Months 1-6: $36k + $3k + $5k + $12k = $56,000/mo
Months 7-12: $57k + $5k + $6k + $25k = $93,000/mo
Months 13-18: $98k + $7k + $6k + $25k = $136,000/mo

TOTAL 18-MONTH BURN: ($56k × 6) + ($93k × 6) + ($136k × 6)
= $336k + $558k + $816k = $1,710,000
```

```

RUNWAY CALCULATION

Starting Cash: $50,000 (bootstrapped to date)

Raise Amount: $750,000

Total Cash: $800,000

Monthly Burn (Average): $95,000/mo

Runway = $800,000 ÷ $95,000 = 8.4 months

PROBLEM: Runway too short.

ADJUSTED PLAN:
• Reduce headcount growth (delay hires to months 8-10)
• Lower marketing spend until $25k MRR
• Target raise: $1.2M (not $750k)

NEW RUNWAY:
$1,250,000 total cash ÷ $75,000/mo avg burn = 16.7 months ✅
```

```
BEAR / BASE / BULL CASES

BEAR CASE (8% MoM growth)
Assumption: Churn increases to 7%, CAC rises to $3,500
Result:
• Month 18 MRR: $45,000
• Month 18 customers: 150
• Revenue vs base: -37%

BASE CASE (15% MoM growth)
Assumption: 5% churn, $2,500 CAC
Result:

```

```
SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

KEY LEVERS (Impact on Month 18 MRR)

1. Growth Rate (±5% MoM)
   - 10% MoM: $48k MRR (-50%)
   - 15% MoM: $96k MRR (base)
   - 20% MoM: $192k MRR (+100%)

   - 3% churn: $115k MRR (+20%)
   - 5% churn: $96k MRR (base)

3. ARPU (±20%)
   - $240/mo: $77k MRR (-20%)
   - $300/mo: $96k MRR (base)
   - $360/mo: $115k MRR (+20%)
   IMPACT: ±$19k per ±$60 ARPU

MOST SENSITIVE: Growth rate
```

## Quality Checklists

### Pitch Deck Review Checklist

Before sending to investors, verify:

**Story & Flow**
- [ ] Problem is painful and expensive ( quantify the cost)
- [ ] Solution is clearly differentiated (not "we're better")
- [ ] Market size is bottom-up (not "we'll get 1% of $10B")
- [ ] Traction is specific (no "thousands of users")
- [ ] Team is relevant to the problem (not "world-class")

**Numbers**
- [ ] Every number matches the source of truth
- [ ] Market size shows TAM → SAM → SOM
- [ ] Revenue model shows ARPU × customers
- [ ] Financial projections include assumptions
- [ ] Use of funds sums to 100%

**Design**
- [ ] Each slide fits on one screen (no scrolling)
- [ ] Font size ≥ 24pt for body text
- [ ] Max 6 bullets per slide
- [ ] One chart per slide (max)
- [ ] Logo and contact info on every slide

**Defensibility**
- [ ] Can explain every number if challenged
- [ ] Can show work for projections
- [ ] Can verify all claims (data, customers, team)
- [ ] Can articulate moat (not "we're first")

---

### Financial Model Review Checklist

Before sharing the model:

**Assumptions**
- [ ] All assumptions are explicit (not buried)
- [ ] Growth rate is defensible (based on traction or comparables)
- [ ] Churn is realistic (not 0%, not 20% for SaaS)
- [ ] CAC/LTV are calculated correctly

**Math**
- [ ] Revenue layers sum correctly
- [ ] Expenses match headcount plan
- [ ] Runway = cash ÷ burn
- [ ] Use of funds matches headcount + timeline

**Scenarios**
- [ ] Bear case: What if growth is 50% slower?
- [ ] Base case: 15% MoM growth (or based on traction)
- [ ] Bull case: What if a key lever goes right?

**Sensitivities**
- [ ] Test ±10% on growth rate
- [ ] Test ±2% on churn
- [ ] Test ±20% on ARPU
- [ ] Identify which lever has most impact

---

### One-Pager Review Checklist

**Content**
- [ ] One sentence explaining what you do
- [ ] Specific traction numbers (MRR, growth, customers)

**Design**
- [ ] Fits on one page (no shrinking font to 8pt)
- [ ] Hierarchy is clear (headings, bullets, spacing)
- [ ] Logo prominent but not overwhelming
- [ ] Contact info easy to find

**Consistency**
- [ ] Numbers match the deck
- [ ] Team roles match the memo
- [ ] Ask matches use of funds
- [ ] No conflicting claims
