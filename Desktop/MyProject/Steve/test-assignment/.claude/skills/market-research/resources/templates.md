# Market Research Templates and Examples

## Research Output Templates

### Generic Research Report Structure

```markdown
# [Research Topic]

## Executive Summary
[2-3 sentence summary of findings and recommendation]

## Key Findings
### Finding 1: [Headline]
- **Evidence:** [Source, date]
- **Implication:** [What this means for the decision]
- **Confidence:** [High/Medium/Low]

### Finding 2: [Headline]
[Repeat for each key finding]

## Implications
[What the findings collectively imply for the decision at hand]

## Risks and Caveats
### Risk 1: [Description]
- **Probability:** [High/Medium/Low]
- **Impact:** [How this would change the conclusion]
- **Mitigation:** [What to watch for]

### Risk 2: [Description]
[Repeat for each risk]

## Recommendation
[Clear, actionable recommendation based on evidence]

**Alternative:** [What to do if recommendation cannot be followed]

## Sources
1. [Source 1] - URL/Reference - Date accessed
2. [Source 2] - URL/Reference - Date accessed
[Continue for all sources]
```

### Market Sizing Template

```markdown

## Executive Summary
[TAM/SAM/SOM numbers, recommendation for pursuit]

- **Definition:** [Who is the entire universe of potential customers?]
- **Calculation:** [Number of entities] × [Average spend]

- [Source 1 - e.g., "Gartner: CRM market $80B by 2025"]
- [Source 2 - e.g., "IBISWorld: 500k US businesses with sales teams"]
- [Date accessed: [Date]]

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):**
- **Definition:** [Segment of TAM your tech/business model can serve]
- **Calculation:** [Number of entities in segment] × [Average spend]
- **Estimate:** [$Y billion]

**Assumptions:**
- [Assumption 1 - e.g., "Only SaaS companies, not all businesses"]
- [Assumption 2 - e.g., "Only companies using cloud CRM"]
- [Assumption 3 - e.g., "US + EU only, not global"]

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):**
- **Definition:** [Portion of SAM you can realistically capture]
- **Calculation:** [Market share target] × [SAM]
- **Estimate:** [$Z million]

**Assumptions:**
- [Assumption 1 - e.g., "5% market share in 5 years"]
- [Assumption 2 - e.g., "Focus on US/EU SMB first"]
- [Assumption 3 - e.g., "Competitors have 80% share, we take 5%"]

**Year 1:**
- [Customers acquired] × [ACV] = [Revenue]

- [Cumulative customers] × [ACV] = [Revenue]

- [Cumulative customers] × [ACV] = [Revenue]

**Historical Growth:**

**CAGR:** [%] over [N] years

**Source:** [Industry report, analyst coverage] - [Date]

**Projected Growth:**

- [Year 5]: [$Y billion]
- CAGR: [%]

**Source:** [Forecast report] - [Date]

**Confidence:** [High/Medium/Low] - [Why]

## Competitive Intensity

- [Leader 2]: [%] market share - [Company name]
- [Others]: [%] - Fragmented
```

## Quick Reference: Research Sources by Type

### Investor Research Sources

**Primary Sources:**
- Fund website (thesis, portfolio, team)
- Crunchbase / PitchBook (funding data)
- LinkedIn (partner backgrounds, recent hires)
- SEC filings (Form D for fund sizes)
- Medium / Substack (partner essays, thesis)

**Secondary Sources:**
- TechCrunch / VentureBeat (fund announcements)
- Podcast interviews (partners on shows)
- Conference talks (partner presentations)
- Twitter/X (partner activity, thesis signals)

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- No updates to portfolio in 12+ months
- Partners leaving fund
- Slow decision-making reputation (check founder forums)
- Portfolio company failures

### Competitive Analysis Sources

**Primary Sources:**
- Company website (product pages, pricing)
- Product documentation (features, limitations)
- G2 / Capterra / Gartner Peer Insights (customer reviews)
- Demo videos / screenshots (product reality)

**Secondary Sources:**
- LinkedIn (hiring velocity, team size)
- Job postings (reveals priorities, tech stack)
- Crunchbase (funding history, investors)
- Built In / AngelList (employee reviews)
- Reddit (r/SaaS, r/startups - unfiltered opinions)
- Competitor comparison sites (G2, TrustRadius)

**Signals to Watch:**
- Product quality: Read 1-star reviews on G2
- Traction: Check LinkedIn employee growth (6-month delta)
- Pricing changes: Use Wayback Machine to see historical pricing
- Feature gaps: Check "upcoming" or "beta" in docs

### Technology Research Sources

**Official Sources:**
- Official documentation (features, limitations)
- GitHub / GitLab (stars, contributors, commit velocity)
- Changelog / release notes (recent activity)
- Security advisories (vulnerabilities)
- Terms of service / SLA (guarantees)

**Community Sources:**
- Stack Overflow (questions, answered ratio)
- Reddit (r/programming, r/devops - opinions)
- Hacker News (discussions, Show HN)
- Dev.to / Medium (tutorials, blog posts)
- Conference talks (YouTube)

**Adoption Signals:**
- GitHub stars (correlates with awareness, not quality)
- npm / PyPI downloads (actual usage)
- Stack Overflow questions (correlates with pain points)

**Warning Signs:**
- No commits in 6+ months (abandoned?)
- More open issues than closed (maintenance burden?)
- No security updates in 12+ months (risk?)
- Documentation outdated (neglected?)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. Confirmation Bias

**Mistake:** Only researching sources that confirm your thesis.

**Fix:** Actively seek disconfirming evidence.
- If you think the market is huge, find sources that say it's small
- If you think a competitor is weak, find sources that say they're strong
- Include a "Counterarguments" section in every research brief

### 2. Stale Data

**Mistake:** Using 3-year-old market reports without noting currency.

**Fix:** Always date-stamp sources.
- Flag data >18 months old as "stale"
- Prioritize sources <6 months old
- If you must use old data, explicitly state why

### 3. Research Theater

**Mistake:** Gathering data without a decision to make.

**Fix:** Start with the decision, then work backward.
- Bad: "Research the AI market"
- Good: "Should we enter the AI market? What's the SOM and competitive intensity?"

### 4. Confusing Fact and Inference

**Mistake:** Presenting guesses as facts.

**Fix:** Label claims clearly.
- **Fact:** "Competitor has 50 employees" (sourced from LinkedIn)
- **Inference:** "Competitor has ~$1M ARR" (estimate based on 50 employees × $20k ARR/employee)
- **Recommendation:** "Given their scale, we can compete on speed"

### 5. Missing the Source

**Mistake:** Presenting numbers without attribution.

**Fix:** Every number gets a source.
- "$10B market" → "Gartner 2023 CRM Report"
- "Competitor raised $5M" → "Crunchbase, Series A announcement"
- "500k customers" → "Company website, homepage claim"

### 6. Ignoring Tail Risk

**Mistake:** Best-case only, no downside.

**Fix:** Always include risks.
- What if the market is 50% smaller?
- What if the competitor responds aggressively?
- What if the technology is abandoned?

## Quality Checklist for All Research

Before delivering any research output, verify:

### [ ] Sourcing
- [ ] Every claim has a source URL or reference
- [ ] Sources are dated (within 6 months if possible)
- [ ] Old data (>18 months) is flagged as "stale"
- [ ] Estimates are labeled as estimates, not facts

### [ ] Balance
- [ ] Contrarian evidence is included
- [ ] Counterarguments are addressed
- [ ] Risks and caveats are explicit
- [ ] Not just confirmation of user's thesis

### [ ] Decision Support
- [ ] Executive summary answers "so what?"
- [ ] Recommendation is clear and actionable
- [ ] Alternative paths are outlined
- [ ] Risk/reward trade-off is explicit

### [ ] Clarity
- [ ] Fact, inference, and recommendation are separated
- [ ] Numbers are contextualized (not just "$10B" but "$10B TAM, $100M SOM")
- [ ] Technical jargon is explained for non-technical audiences
- [ ] Structure follows the template for readability
