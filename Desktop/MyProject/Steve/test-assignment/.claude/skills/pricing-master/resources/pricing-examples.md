## B2B SaaS Examples

### HubSpot - Tiered Pricing Excellence

**Structure:**
- Free: CRM tools, contact management
- Starter: $20/mo - Marketing basics
- Professional: $890/mo - Marketing automation
- Enterprise: $3,200/mo - Custom + reporting

**Why It Works:**
1. Clear feature differentiation between tiers
2. Natural progression as businesses grow
3. Enterprise tier creates anchor effect
4. Bundles related products (marketing + sales + service)

**Upgrade Triggers:**
- Contact list limits (Free: 1M contacts, but limited features)
- Automation workflows (Starter → Professional)
- Reporting & attribution (Professional → Enterprise)
- Advanced permissions (Enterprise)

**Key Metrics:**
- ARPU: ~$1,100/month
- Net Revenue Retention: 110%+
- Gross Margin: ~80%

### Stripe - Developer-First Usage Pricing

**Structure:**
- No base fee
- 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction
- Premium add-ons:
  - Radar (fraud): $0.02/transaction
  - Terminal (in-person): 2.7% + $0.05
  - Instant payouts: 1% fee

**Why It Works:**
1. Aligns with customer revenue
2. Low barrier to entry
3. Scales automatically with business growth
4. Add-ons target specific use cases

**Smart Moves:**
- Started with simple per-transaction pricing
- Added premium features over time
- Expanded into adjacent products (Billing, Capital)
- Enterprise tier for large merchants

**Key Metrics:**
- Gross Margin: ~45% (lower due to payment processing)
- Volume per customer: Scales infinitely
- Net Revenue Retention: 125%+

## AI/ML Product Examples

### OpenAI - Credit-Based Pioneer

**Structure:**
- Pay-as-you-go credits
- gpt-4o: $2.50/million input tokens
- gpt-4o-mini: $0.15/million input tokens
- Tiered pricing based on model capability

**Why It Works:**
1. Transparent pricing per usage
2. Different models for different cost/benefit
3. Volume discounts for high usage
4. Prepaid model improves cash flow

**Credit System Design:**
```
Cost Calculation:
- 1,000 tokens ≈ 750 words
- gpt-4o-mini: $0.15 per 1M tokens
- Processing a 300-page book (~150K words) = $0.03
- Processing 1M customer support tickets = $30-150
```

**Key Metrics:**
- Gross Margin: Estimated 50-60% (inference costs)
- Volume: Scales with adoption
- Customer retention: High due to switching costs

### GitHub - Per-Seat Collaboration

**Structure:**
- Free: Public/private repos
- Pro: $4/user/mo - Individual developers
- Team: $4/user/mo - Team features
- Enterprise: $21/user/mo - SSO, audit, support

**Why It Works:**
1. Low price per seat reduces friction
2. Clear distinction between individual and team
3. Enterprise features justify 5x price difference
4. Network effects create lock-in

**Upgrade Triggers:**
- Private repos for teams (Free → Team)
- SSO and security (Team → Enterprise)
- Compliance and support (Enterprise)
- Advanced code security (Enterprise add-ons)

**Key Metrics:**
- ARPU: ~$10-15/user/month
- Gross Margin: ~85%+
- Retention: 95%+ (platform lock-in)

## Common Patterns Across Success Stories

### 1. Clear Upgrade Triggers
Every successful pricing strategy has natural progression:
- Usage limits (storage, bandwidth, minutes)
- Team features (collaboration, permissions)
- Enterprise features (SSO, security, compliance)

### 2. Anchor Pricing
- Highest tier makes middle tiers feel reasonable
- Enterprise tier justifies Professional pricing
- "Most Popular" badge guides choice

### 3. Low Friction Entry
- Free trials or generous free tiers
- No credit card required initially
- Self-service onboarding

### 4. Value-Based Pricing
- Price correlates with customer value
- Not based on cost-plus pricing
- Captures willingness to pay

### 5. Hybrid Models
Most companies combine pricing models:
- Base subscription + usage overages
- Per-seat + API usage
- Freemium + tiered paid plans

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start Simple** - All successful companies started with 2-3 tiers
2. **Add Complexity Gradually** - Enterprise features and usage pricing come later
3. **Protect Margins** - Usage limits and overages prevent margin erosion
4. **Create Upgrade Paths** - Each tier should create natural desire to upgrade
5. **Test Continuously** - All pricing should be A/B tested
6. **Evolve with Market** - Pricing changes as product matures and competition emerges
